Background

Identifying relevant funding opportunities remains a significant challenge for faculty at primarily undergraduate institutions. Time constraints and the breadth of available grants often result in missed opportunities aligned with researchers' expertise.

This project evaluated the implementation of Atom Grants, an AI-powered grant discovery platform, at Minot State University — tracking whether lightweight, AI-assisted discovery could drive meaningful faculty adoption within a short rollout window.

Methods

  • Cohort: 202 faculty and staff provisioned across all MSU departments.
  • Window: 90 days following the October 30, 2025 onboarding session.
  • Metrics: user activation, email open / click behavior, grant page views, saves (favorites), search queries, AI chat sessions, and content preferences by funder type and grant category.
  • Benchmarking: compared against Atom Grants' cross-institution platform benchmark and published email-engagement industry averages.
  • Attribution: every grant view tagged with its referring channel (email, dashboard, search, direct) for acquisition analysis.

Conclusions & Next Steps

"

AI-assisted grant discovery can achieve meaningful faculty adoption within a short implementation timeline at a primarily undergraduate institution. High activation (58%), exceptional email engagement (94%), and a hockey-stick trajectory of monthly visits (85 → 200+) indicate the platform effectively lowers the barriers that typically keep faculty from discovering aligned funding.

The 180-day review will evaluate whether elevated discovery activity translates into submitted applications — the metric that ultimately determines institutional ROI for AI-assisted funding tools at PUIs. Early pipeline signals (72 favorited grants concentrated in disability, health, and engineering education) support an optimistic hypothesis.
