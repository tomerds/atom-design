What's changing

In the first 90 days, NIH accounted for 52% of all grant views. Since Jan 21, that share has dropped to 32% — not because NIH interest declined, but because everything else grew faster.

Six funders that received zero views in the first period (DoD, Roche, Gilead, AcademyHealth, and others) are now in the top 12. This is the maturation signal: researchers trust the platform enough to discover unfamiliar funders, not just confirm the ones they already know.

Next steps

Three threads for the next quarter.

01

Activate the 922.

Nearly a thousand NYULH profiles have never opened a grant page. A targeted first-touch campaign — department-specific, single high-relevance grant per email — is the highest-leverage move for Q3.

02

Publish the NYULH case study.

The first six months are a strong public story, and we don't have one yet from NYULH. We'd draft it together — a short interview, the headline numbers, your sign-off — and run it alongside the other partner case studies on atomgrants.com.

03

Open renewal conversations.

The current term renews on August 31. We'd like to start the conversation now: scope, stakeholders, and what an expanded Atom partnership could include. Two new modules are coming online (see next slide).

What's coming · summer 2026

Two new modules, built on the same engine.

The first six months proved the discovery layer. The next phase extends Atom into the workflows that follow once a researcher finds the right opportunity — finding collaborators, and getting from match to submitted proposal.

Module 01 · Discovery

Researcher Search

A purpose-built directory of NYULH faculty, indexed by funding history, publications, and grant interests. Find a co-investigator, a methods lead, or the right senior PI in seconds, without leaving Atom.

  • Search by expertise, funder track record, or open call
  • Surface internal collaborators relevant to a specific grant
  • Discover external collaborators across a worldwide database of researchers
Module 02 · Submission

Proposals

Everything between "I'm interested" and "submitted." Three components, designed to be used independently or together.

  • Proposal guides — funder-specific playbooks, sections, examples
  • Proposal management — track drafts, deadlines, collaborators, status
  • Proposal review & drafting — AI-assisted writing and structured feedback
